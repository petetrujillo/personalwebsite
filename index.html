Purdue M.S. Capstone

Explainable AI Tutor & Governance Framework

Explainable AI (XAI) AI Product Strategy RAG AI Governance (EdTech)

The Problem

Standard LLM-based tutors are "black boxes." They can't explain *how* they arrive at a score, and they are prone to hallucination, making them unsuitable and unsafe for middle school students. Educators and parents need a system that is transparent, trustworthy, and constrained to an approved curriculum.

My Role

As my master's capstone, I designed, built, and documented the entire system. This involved full-stack development, multi-agent LLM orchestration, RAG implementation, and creating a comprehensive governance framework to ensure safety and compliance.

The Solution

I built a full-stack application that uses a multi-agent LLM system (Evaluator, Tutor, Subject Matter Expert) to provide explainable feedback. All agents are constrained by a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) model, ensuring their responses are based *only* on approved curriculum data. The system delivers a transparent, rubric-based score (XAI) and provides "human-in-the-loop" oversight for educators, all while adhering to FERPA and COPPA compliance.

Key Skills Demonstrated

Explainable AI (XAI)

Designed a system to provide transparent, rubric-based scoring, solving the "black box" problem.

LLM Multi-Agent Systems

Orchestrated multiple AI agents (Evaluator, Tutor, SME) to collaborate on a complex task.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Constrained LLM output to a secure, approved knowledge base to prevent hallucination.

AI Governance (EdTech)

Built-in compliance for educational regulations (FERPA, COPPA) and user-centric safety controls.

AI Policy Research

AI's Role in Wildfire Mitigation

AI Governance NIST AI RMF Public Sector Tech Stakeholder Analysis

The Problem

California's wildfire management is dangerously fragmented, with responsibility split between State (CalFire), Federal (US Forest Service), and six independent County agencies. This fractured system creates inconsistent AI policies, redundant spending, and uncoordinated technology deployment, leaving the state vulnerable to rising wildfire costs ($3.1B+ annually).

My Role

I authored this policy paper to analyze the systemic risks of this fragmented governance. I developed actionable recommendations to centralize and standardize the state's approach to AI in wildfire mitigation.

The Solution

My core recommendation is to formally empower a single agency, the Office of Wildfire Technology Research and Development (OWTRD), to act as the central authority for all AI technology evaluation. This centralization would be guided by two new mandates:

  • Adopt a Formal Risk Framework: Require all new AI technologies to be evaluated using the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) to ensure safety, fairness, and accountability.
  • Prioritize Advanced Tech: Direct the OWTRD to shift from simple monitoring to evaluating advanced AI systems, such as autonomous drone fleets and "Digital Twin" predictive models.

Key Skills Demonstrated

AI Governance & Risk (NIST RMF)

Applied the NIST AI RMF as a practical solution to a large-scale public sector challenge.

Stakeholder Analysis

Mapped a complex 10+ group stakeholder ecosystem to identify the root cause of policy failure.

Technology Assessment

Analyzed the specific risks/benefits of drones and Digital Twins and created detailed mitigation plans.

Public Sector Strategy

Drafted a formal, actionable policy recommendation for a state-level agency.

Policy Brief

California's AI Energy Problem

AI Policy Sustainability Policy Framework Design AI Ethics

The Problem

California's official energy forecasts (2022-2023) are dangerously outdated, failing to account for the post-2023 surge in generative AI energy demand. This creates a critical gap between projected supply and actual demand, threatening state clean energy goals. The root cause is a lack of data, as private AI developers treat their energy consumption as a trade secret.

My Role

As the sole author, I conducted the analysis and developed a proactive governance framework to address this systemic risk, targeting stakeholders like the CA Governor's Office and the CA Energy Commission.

The Solution

I designed the "Blueprint for Powering California's AI Future (BP2030)", a policy framework built on four principles:

  • Partnership & Transparency: Establish voluntary data-sharing partnerships between AI developers, utilities, and state agencies.
  • Measurement & Ratings: Create a public, "Energy Star"-style rating system for AI model efficiency.
  • Innovation: Provide tax credits for developing and sharing energy-efficient AI techniques.
  • Efficiency & Learning: Implement a continuous "Learning Loop" to formally update state energy forecasts with this new data.

Key Skills Demonstrated

AI Policy & Governance

Authored a complete, state-level governance framework from the ground up.

Horizon Scanning & Risk Analysis

Identified a critical blind spot in state policy by synthesizing private-sector and academic data.

Stakeholder Analysis

Designed a solution that creates value for all parties (government, industry, and utilities).

Data-Driven Recommendation

Used quantitative analysis to illustrate the gap between public forecasts and private-sector projections.

"Memorable Moments" AI Pitch

AI Risk Management Computer Vision Business Case CX Strategy

The Problem

In the cruise industry, guest satisfaction and "memorable moments" are the single biggest predictors of loyalty. However, creating these moments is often left to chance. This is an inefficient, unscalable way to manage a core business driver.

My Role

I designed this product concept and business case to demonstrate how AI could be leveraged to proactively *create* these high-value moments, moving guest satisfaction from a reactive metric to a proactive strategy.

The Solution

An on-ship system using existing security cameras and machine vision (CNNs) to identify guests. The system instantly cross-references the guest's photo with their profile, sending relevant details (e.g., "Lisa, Birthday Today") to a staff-facing "Crew App." This allows staff to deliver hyper-personalized service, directly increasing loyalty. The pitch included a 45-day pilot program to measure the direct lift in "Likelihood to Rebook" and "Likelihood to Recommend" KPIs, along with a proactive assessment of privacy and ethical risks.

Key Skills Demonstrated

AI Product Strategy

Monarque Solutions ROI Calculator

Business Case & ROI Value Quantification Consulting

The Problem

Monarque Solutions, a caregiver consulting firm, faced a common sales challenge: their services are deeply personal and essential, but their financial value isn't immediately obvious to prospects. They needed a way to instantly communicate a compelling Return on Investment (ROI) to potential clients visiting their website.

My Role

As a pro bono consultant, I worked with the firm to understand their value proposition and client pain points. I then designed and built a simple, interactive ROI calculator to serve as a lead-generation tool on their website.

The Solution

The solution is a lightweight, web-based calculator. It prompts visitors to answer a few simple questions about their situation (e.g., hours spent on caregiving, out-of-pocket costs). Based on their inputs, it instantly quantifies the potential financial savings and time recovered by using Monarque's services. This approach transforms a vague value proposition into a hard, personalized number, creating a powerful incentive for the visitor to engage with the sales team.
